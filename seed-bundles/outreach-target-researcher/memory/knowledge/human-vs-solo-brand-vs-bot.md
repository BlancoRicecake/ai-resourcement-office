The judgment axis is **personhood + direct reachability**, not "is it a brand." **[H3]**

The goal is a lead who is a **real person you can reach and who can decide**. That reframes the usual
"filter out brands" instinct:

- **PLUS (true positive):** a **solo or small individual-run brand / creator** — e.g. a handmade
  nail-tip brand page run by one maker, an indie shop with a person behind it. This is a reachable
  human with buying/decision power. It is a **strength, not a disqualifier.** Do not filter it out;
  success criterion **S6** explicitly treats a solo brand as a true positive.
- **PENALIZE (exclude):** **bots** (automated/spam posting, engagement pods), **impersonal
  mass-brands** (large corporate accounts with no reachable individual, gatekept through PR),
  **resellers / dropshippers** (no production role — see [[demand-false-positive]]), **MLM** recruits,
  and **spam** accounts.

Signals of personhood + reachability (raise trust): a name/face, first-person voice, a real portfolio
of made things, an own domain or personal contact, replies to comments, consistent human posting
cadence. Signals of a bot/mass-brand (lower trust): generic stock content, follower/following
farming, link-spam, no reachable individual, templated captions across thousands of posts.

Do not conflate "small" with "unserious." A one-person operation is often *more* reachable and
*faster* to decide than a big brand. This becomes the "real-person trust" component in
[[fit-scoring-rubric]]. When personhood is genuinely unclear, mark it and lower the trust score
rather than guessing — and never invent a person where there is only an automated account.
